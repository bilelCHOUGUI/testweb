    Skikda District Briefly

    Skikda, formerly Philippeville, town, Mediterranean Sea port, northeastern Algeria, situated on the Gulf of Stora. Founded by French Marshal Sylvain-Charles Valée in 1838 as the port of Constantine, it has an artificial harbour. Skikda occupies the site of ancient Rusicade, port of 4th-century Cirta, and has the largest Roman theatre in Algeria (used as a quarry, this has been greatly depleted). Roman artifacts are in the local museum.

    Short Introduction of the City

    The Wilaya of Skikda comprises thirteen (13) dairates grouping together thirty eight (38) municipalities and covers an area of ​​4,137.68 km² with 130 km of coastline. COLO, Located in the western part of the Wilaya of Skikda, it is a highly touristic region with its sandy beaches, its panoramic sites with breathtaking views of the sea, its historical remains of great historical and cultural value. The bay of Collo is considered one of the most beautiful in Algeria, the region also contains an imposing forest massif where mountain tourism, sports, hiking, discovery and hunting can develop. Collo l'Antique Chullu is today an important fishing port located 71 km from the capital of Wilaya. Its fine sandy beaches: Tamanart, Baie des jeunes filles, Teleza, Benzouit, offer vacationers a splendid setting for relaxation and escape. STORA, Former Phoenician then Roman counter, fishing and marina port, fine sandy beaches and various seaside infrastructures. The Corniche de Stora offers walkers over three (03) km a breathtaking view of the sea and the horizon. You can also admire the former refuge of the Phoenician galleys, the island of Serigina, a stone's throw from the coast and the imposing lighthouse that stands there, an ancient landmark for old ships in search of berth. .BEN M'HIDI (LES PLATANES), Fine sandy beaches as far as the eye can see, over 10 km to Mount Fil-Fila, from where you can admire the sunsets on summer evenings. LA MARSA, a seaside resort located 65 km east of the Wilaya, with its beaches of rare beauty, has a fishing and marina port and a diversity of flora and fauna proliferating in favor of a vast wetland made up of rivers and lakes favorable to the development of scientific tourism, fishing, hunting and sports.
